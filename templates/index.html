Stellar evolution

Stellar evolution is a description of the way that stars change with time.

On human timescales, most stars do not appear to change at all, but if we were to look for billions of years, we would see how stars are born, how they age, and finally how they die.

These processes describe the formation of lighter elements in stars via nuclear fusion. But heavier elements are not thermodynamically feasible to synthesise, because the increase in binding energy makes their fusion endothermic.

So how do we make these?

Heavy element formation
